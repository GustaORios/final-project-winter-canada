Capilano and Grouse Mountain

Experience the coastal mountains near Vancouver: suspension bridge walks, gondolas, and panoramic winter views.

Evenings glow with festive lights; daytime offers forest trails and alpine activities just a short ride from the city.

Skiing & Snowboarding

Chase crisp air and fresh powder across Canada’s winter resorts—from beginner-friendly greens to backcountry adventures.

Safety, skill progression, and layered clothing keep you comfortable and confident on the slopes.

  • Gear: helmet, goggles, insulated gloves.
  • Layers: base, mid, shell to regulate warmth.
  • Practice: lessons accelerate improvement.

Northern Lights

On frigid, clear nights, the sky may dance with auroras—bundle up, find dark skies, and be patient with the cold.

Use red-filtered lights to preserve night vision and pack warm drinks to enjoy the celestial show.

Barbecues (in every season)

Canadians grill in snow, sun, and everything between—keep a shovel handy, preheat longer, and savor that smoky flavor.

  • Setup: clear snow, shield from wind.
  • Timing: compensate for cold air temps.
  • Food: hearty cuts, root veggies, hot cocoa.

Sleep

Shorter days can nudge sleep cycles. Keep a consistent schedule, seek daylight, and cool your bedroom slightly.

  • Routine: fixed bedtime, wind-down ritual.
  • Light: bright mornings, dim evenings.
  • Comfort: breathable layers and humidification.

FAQ

What's the best time to see the Northern Lights?
Look for clear, dark skies during the coldest months and away from city lights. Patience and layers help a lot.
Can I barbecue in the snow safely?
Yes—clear snow around the grill, block wind, and allow extra preheat time. Keep a fire extinguisher nearby.
How do I stay warm on the slopes?
Dress in layers, protect extremities, and take warm breaks. Consider lessons to reduce time spent idle in lifts.
